BP MS 150 Cycling Kit

Overview

RigUp team members participated in the BP MS 150, a two-day fundraising bike ride from Houston to Austin for multiple sclerosis, and they need a branded cycling kit. The goal of this project is to create a unisex design that represents the company. My role was to research what makes a good cycling kit, design the cycling kit, and communicate with the production company.

  • Research


    When I received this project, I knew nothing about cycling attires. In order to create the best end-product, I must explore and learn about this realm of the subject. Therefore, I talked to an avid cyclist, created an inspiration board, and analyzed award-winning designs.

  • Findings

    Some of the things that I learned are designs on the cycling kit need to consider the way the fabrics are pieced together, design needs to flatter the movement of the cyclist, and award-winning kits are unique in their placement of graphics.

  • Design

    The sides are darker than the body to accentuate the natural curves of the body and to give a slimmer look. It elongates to the underarm in order to dim out any potential sweat spots. The little RigUp logos are to add some detailing to the kit overall.

  • Results


  • The cyclists on the day received many compliments and were told that they stood out during the ride. Some people even asked if they can buy the kit. The kit gives a modern twist to the traditional sponsored kit where there were an overwhelming amount of colors and logos.
