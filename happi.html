HAPPI
(Health App for People with Preventative Issues)

Our team created HAPPI (Health App for People with Preventative Issues), a new tool for people who want to find local support group meetings for coping with their issues. Whether someone is struggling with addiction, loss, or health issues, they can use HAPPI to find people who can relate to their struggles. My role was to come up with the strategy.

  • Problem


    Michael and Susan Dell gave $50 million grant to develop Dell Medical School at University of Texas at Austin, and they aim to improve access and quality of healthcare in the Travis County area.

  • Context


    The Roden Medical Innovation Panel has asked us to propose a plan for the implementation of a single new technology that will reduce inefficiencies that exist within the medical landscape. Alternatively, the panel will accept a modified version of an existing technology to be implemented in an innovative way.

  • Process


    The uncertainty of advanced technology led us to doubt the reality of the solution. We can estimate how great or successful the technology is but at the end of the day, it will be extremely costly and it will take a long time in order to solve these inefficiencies in the hospital/healthcare space. Therefore, we thought of something that is more achievable and feasible.

  • Research


  • Design


  • Result



    Our team placed first place out of 15 teams in the case competition. After speaking with the judges, we learn that we won by a feasible element of our solution in comparison to other teams. Like in our prediction, other teams used advanced technology that we are unsure about to solve the inefficiencies in the medical landscape. The judges loved the idea so much that they encourage us to make this a reality since the implementation is doable.
